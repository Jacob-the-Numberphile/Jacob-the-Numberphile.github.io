Work Experience and Research Projects

CAMCOS Project

I spent a semester working in CAMCOS, an intensive applied mathematics project contracted by Ethereum. The project involved a new pricing mechanism called EIP-1559, which was introduced into the marketplace to solve block congestion and bring transactions on the blockchain. My involvement with the project was acquiring a node to obtain data, cleaning the data, and using various graphs and statistical techniques to visualize and analyze the data. Furthermore, I had to learn a lot about how the Ethereum marketplace works, along with the various terms, jargon, and specific economic behaviors that are associated with a cryptocurrency-based economy. I tested the effectiveness of EIP-1559 by running simulations of the different pricing mechanisms to determine if the intended effect was reached.
